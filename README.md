## About

*The Long Walk* is a browser-based game concept built as a UX/game design prototype for
charity: water. It puts players in the shoes of Amara, a young girl who walks daily to
the nearest water source, a reality millions of people face around the world.

Players jump over obstacles (rocks, thorny brush, dry cracked earth) and collect water
drops to keep their energy up, while a distance meter tracks progress toward a real,
commonly cited average walking distance for water access. The goal isn't just to make
a fun game, it's to turn an abstract statistic into something players briefly feel,
then give them a clear way to act on it.

## Play it

- **Tap, click, or press `Space`** to jump
- Collect teal water drops to restore energy
- Avoid obstacles or you'll lose energy
- Reach the distance goal before your energy runs out

## Built with

- Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (no frameworks, no build step)
- HTML5 Canvas for all game rendering and animation
- Hosted with GitHub Pages

## Project background

This was built as a design + development exercise: choosing a game concept, hand-drawing
a mockup, planning the game logic, applying brand guidelines, then building a working
prototype end to end.

## Customization

Nearly everything about the game (distance goal, difficulty, character name, milestone
messages, stat copy) lives in a single `CONFIG` object near the top of the `<script>`
tag in `index.html`. Change values there and refresh to see the effect.
